Physicians spend four and a half hours every day on documentation. More time than they spend with patients.

This is Voice to Health Record, a clinical extraction pipeline powered by Google's MedGemma that transforms voice dictation into structured EHR data automatically.

The problem: clinicians speak naturally, but EHRs require structured data with medical codes. Manual entry is slow and burns out physicians.

The solution: AI-powered extraction that listens to clinical dictation and automatically populates the EHR.

Here's a live demo. A 48-year-old male with chest pain on aspirin. I'll send the clinical note to the extraction API.

In under three seconds, MedGemma extracts the complete picture: diagnosis of angina with ICD-10 code, medications with RxNorm codes, vital signs, and orders for stress testing and statin therapy.

We benchmarked against rule-based extraction on 199 clinical entities. MedGemma achieves 69 percent F1, a 13 point improvement over baseline. Critically, MedGemma catches allergies and family history that traditional rules completely miss.

The system exports to FHIR, CDA, or HL7 for any EHR system. Deploys to the cloud or on-premises with complete HIPAA compliance.

Voice to Health Record. Open source. Because physicians should spend their time with patients, not paperwork.
