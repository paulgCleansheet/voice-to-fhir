Physicians spend four and a half hours every day on documentation. More time than they spend with patients.

This is Voice to FHIR — a proof of concept that uses Google's MedGemma to transform voice dictation into structured EHR data automatically.

The problem: clinicians speak naturally, but EHRs require structured data with medical codes. Manual entry is slow and burns out physicians.

The solution: AI-powered extraction that listens to clinical dictation and automatically populates the EHR.

Here's a live demo. A 48-year-old male with chest pain on aspirin. I'll send the clinical note to the extraction API.

In under three seconds, MedGemma extracts the complete picture: diagnosis of angina with ICD-10 code, medications with RxNorm codes, vital signs, and orders for stress testing and statin therapy.

We benchmarked against rule-based extraction on 199 clinical entities. MedGemma achieves 69 percent F1 — a 13 point improvement over baseline. Critically, MedGemma catches allergies and family history that rules completely miss — zero percent baseline on both.

This is a proof of concept, and 69 percent is honest. But we've mapped the path to production: prompt engineering for orders, ASR preprocessing, and scaling to MedGemma 27B. Our analysis projects 78 to 83 percent F1 — where reviewing extractions becomes faster than entering data from scratch.

The system exports to FHIR, CDA, or HL7 for any EHR system. Deploys to the cloud at three cents per extraction, or on-premises for two thousand dollars — a fraction of annual scribe costs.

Voice to FHIR. Open source. A working proof of concept with a clear path to production. Because physicians should spend their time with patients, not paperwork.
