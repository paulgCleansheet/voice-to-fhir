Voice-to-Health-Record: Clinical Documentation Extraction with MedGemma
Duration: 3 minutes
Voice: Adam or Rachel (Professional Narrator)

---

Physicians spend four and a half hours every day on documentation. More time than they spend with patients.

This is Voice to Health Record, a clinical extraction pipeline powered by Google's Med-Gemma that transforms voice dictation into structured EHR data automatically.

The problem: clinicians speak naturally, but E-H-Rs require structured data with medical codes. Manual entry is slow and burns out physicians.

The solution: A-I-powered extraction that listens to clinical dictation and automatically populates the E-H-R.

Here's a live demo. A 48-year-old male with chest pain on aspirin. I'll send the clinical note to the extraction A-P-I.

In under three seconds, Med-Gemma extracts the complete picture: diagnosis of ANG-uh-nuh with I-C-D-10 code, medications with R-X-Norm codes, vital signs, and orders for stress testing and statin therapy.

We benchmarked against rule-based extraction on 199 clinical entities. Med-Gemma achieves 69 percent F-1, a 13 point improvement over baseline. Critically, Med-Gemma catches allergies and family history that traditional rules completely miss.

The system exports to F-H-I-R, C-D-A, or H-L-7 for any E-H-R system. Deploys to the cloud or on-premises with complete H-I-P-A-A compliance.

Voice to Health Record. Open source. Because physicians should spend their time with patients, not paperwork.
